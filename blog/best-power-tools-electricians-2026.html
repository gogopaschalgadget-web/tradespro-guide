How to Choose the Right Power Tools for Your Electrician Business

Battery Platform First

The most important decision you make before buying a single power tool is which battery platform to commit to. Milwaukee M18, DeWalt 20V MAX, and Makita 18V LXT are the three dominant platforms in professional electrical work. Each has 100+ tools in the ecosystem. Pick one and stick with it. Two Milwaukee drills, an M18 recip saw, and M18 lights on the same battery platform means three fewer battery chargers in the van, and you can share spare packs across tools.

Brushless vs. Brushed Motors

For tools you will use every day, buy brushless. Brushless motors run cooler, last longer, and get significantly more runtime per charge than brushed motors of similar spec. The price premium is typically $30-$50 and pays for itself within six months of daily use. Reserve brushed tools for occasional-use items and apprentice starter kits where budget is the primary concern.

When to Invest in Premium Tools

Buy premium (Milwaukee FUEL, DeWalt XR, Makita LXT Brushless) for your primary drill, impact driver, and recip saw. These are in your hand eight hours a day. Buy mid-tier for your oscillating tool and multi-purpose tools used less frequently. Buy value tier for specialty items used a handful of times per month.

Pro Tip: Buy combo kits from one brand platform when possible. Milwaukee, DeWalt, and Makita all offer drill plus impact driver combos at 20-30% less than buying tools separately. Add batteries and chargers once, then add tools at tool-only pricing going forward.

Frequently Asked Questions

What brand of drill do most electricians use?

Milwaukee M18 FUEL is the most common platform seen on commercial electrical jobsites. DeWalt 20V MAX is the dominant choice for residential work, partly because of its lower cost of entry. Makita holds strong with electricians who prioritize light weight for overhead work. All three platforms are professional-grade.

Do electricians need a cordless drill and an impact driver?

Yes. They serve different purposes. A drill gives you precision and controlled torque for drilling holes and driving screws into delicate materials. An impact driver delivers maximum driving torque for structural screws, lag bolts, and high-resistance fasteners. Most working electricians carry both and switch based on the task.

Is a Fluke multimeter worth the price for electricians?

Yes, for your primary meter. The Fluke 117 costs roughly 2x a budget True-RMS meter, but the accuracy on VFD and dimmer circuits, the NCV detector, and the durability justify the premium. A misread on a live circuit is a safety risk. For a backup or apprentice meter, the Klein MM600 or Ideal 61-340 are solid value alternatives.

What power tools should an apprentice electrician buy first?

Start with four tools: a drill/driver combo kit (includes batteries), an oscillating multi-tool (tool only, borrow a battery), a basic True-RMS multimeter (Klein MM600 or similar), and a voltage tester. Budget around $300-$400 for quality versions of these four. Add an impact driver, clamp meter, and reciprocating saw as your income grows.

Are the Amazon prices in this guide accurate?

Prices fluctuate on Amazon. The ranges shown reflect recent pricing but may change due to sales, stock availability, and promotions. Check the Amazon link for current pricing. Tool-only (bare tool) prices are always lower than kit prices. If you already have compatible batteries, buy tool-only and save $30-$80 per tool.
