HVAC Contractor Marketing: 7 Ways to Get More Calls

Published April 6, 2026 | 10 min read

HVAC contractors face a unique marketing challenge. Most customers don't think about heating and cooling until something breaks. Worse, they expect you to show up immediately and charge premium prices in emergencies. This creates feast-or-famine cash flow if you're not intentional about your marketing.

The solution is to build consistent lead flow before emergencies happen. This guide covers seven proven marketing strategies that HVAC contractors are using right now to get more calls and stabilize revenue in 2026.

Quick Win: The top 20% of HVAC contractors consistently outperform others on one metric: they answer customer calls within 30 seconds. Implementation: hire a part-time call answerer or use an AI assistant. Cost: $500-1500/month. Result: 40-60% more emergency calls converted to jobs.

1. Google Local Services Ads (LSA)

Google Local Services is the fastest way to get phone calls from customers actively searching for HVAC contractors right now. You only pay when a customer calls you, not just for clicks. Most HVAC contractors spend $300-800 monthly to get 5-15 qualified calls.

How it works: Set up a Google Business Profile, apply for Local Services, set your service area, and Google shows your business to customers searching "HVAC near me" or "emergency AC repair." You get verified with a Google badge, which increases trust.

Cost per call: $20-50 depending on your service area and competition. A single HVAC service call averages $150-300. Close rate is 50-70%, so each call is worth $75-210 in profit potential.

Action: Sign up this week. Get 5 positive reviews before launch to improve your ranking. Set a daily budget of $20-30 to test response times and call handling before scaling.

2. Seasonal Service Specials (Spring and Fall)

Most HVAC work clusters around summer and winter extremes. But spring and fall are goldmines for maintenance contracts. Homeowners want AC or heating checked before the season hits.

Strategy: Offer $79-149 seasonal checkups that include a full inspection, filter replacement, and efficiency report. Upsell maintenance plans that cover filter changes and quarterly inspections at a fixed monthly rate ($29-59/month).

A customer on a $39/month maintenance plan generates $468 annually from a single $79 initial service. After 50 maintenance customers, you have $23400 in predictable annual revenue.

Marketing execution: Send postcards to your service area in February and August. Email previous customers offering the special. Post on Facebook and Google Local Services.

Maintenance Plan Template:
  • Spring checkup (April-May): $99
  • Fall checkup (September-October): $99
  • Monthly filter change reminder and 15% discount on repairs: $29/month or free with annual prepay ($199 for both checkups + 6 months service)
  • 24/7 emergency priority scheduling for members

3. Referral Program with Residential Real Estate Agents

Real estate agents are excellent HVAC lead sources. Home inspectors flag HVAC issues during inspections. Agents refer contractors to fix problems before closing, or after sale during repairs and upgrades.

How to recruit agents: Identify the top 10 real estate agents in your service area (check MLS or local real estate websites). Call them directly. Offer $150-250 cash for each referral that becomes a job. Provide them with service cards and your direct number.

Most agents refer 2-3 HVAC jobs annually. Even at $200 per referral, 10 agents refer 20-30 jobs per year worth $3000-6000 in new business.

Tip: Also build relationships with home inspectors and property managers. They have the same need.

4. YouTube Video Marketing

HVAC customers search YouTube for videos on "why is my AC not cooling" and "furnace making noise." These are high-intent searches. Create simple how-to videos showing basic troubleshooting, then include your phone number in the video description.

Easy video ideas:

  • How to change your furnace filter (3-4 min)
  • How to clean your AC condenser unit (5 min)
  • 5 signs your AC needs repair (4 min)
  • Why your thermostat isn't working (3 min)
  • Furnace maintenance checklist (4 min)

Upload using your business name and service area in the title. Include a description with your phone number, website, and service area zip codes. Run a small Google ad campaign ($200-500) driving traffic to your best video.

One viral video (5000+ views) can generate 5-15 calls from video viewers. Total cost to produce, upload, and promote: $300-800.

5. Facebook and Instagram Ads Targeting Homeowners

Facebook ads are cost-effective for HVAC contractors. Target homeowners in your service area aged 35-65 (the primary home decision makers) with ads promoting seasonal checkups, maintenance plans, or emergency service.

Campaign structure:

  • Budget: $300-500/month to test
  • Target: homeowners, single family home owners, engaged audience
  • Ad creative: photo of your team, customer testimonial, or video walkthrough of a job
  • Call-to-action: Book appointment, call now, or learn more about maintenance plans
  • Landing page: Simple form collecting name, email, phone, service needed

Expected results: 50-100 ad clicks monthly generating 5-15 qualified leads. Cost per lead: $20-50. If 20% convert to jobs at $200-400 average value, you break even at $2000-4000 monthly revenue.

6. Local Business Directory and Map Listings

Make sure you're listed on Google Maps, Yelp, Apple Maps, Angie's List, and The Home Depot Service Marketplace. Many customers start searches on these platforms.

Quick wins:

  • Ensure your Google Business Profile is complete with photos, hours, service area, and appointment booking
  • Get 50+ Google reviews. Offer a $25 gift card for a verified Google review
  • Use Yelp Ads ($5-15 per call) to appear at the top of Yelp search for your service area
  • Get certified on The Home Depot Service Marketplace (free) to get referrals from DIY customers

7. Partnership with HVAC Supply Companies

Local HVAC supply shops know contractors but also work with homeowners buying equipment. Ask the supply company owner if you can post a flyer offering free seasonal checkups or commission referrals.

Some supply companies also recommend local contractors to customers. Building a relationship with the supply manager can result in 3-5 steady referrals monthly.

The Marketing Calendar for HVAC

January-March: Run spring maintenance ads and emails. Post heating maintenance content. Focus on Google Local Services.

April-May: Offer spring AC checkups. Send postcards. Reach out to real estate agents about peak home buying season.

June-August: Maximize emergency service calls. Maintain Google Local Services. Post YouTube videos on air conditioning troubleshooting.

September-November: Run fall maintenance ads. Focus on furnace checkups. Email and postcard campaigns for heating season.

December: Holiday cards and emails to previous customers. Highlight maintenance plans and emergency 24/7 service availability.

Expected Results (First 90 Days)

If you implement three of these strategies with a total marketing budget of $500-800 monthly, expect:

  • 20-40 new customer inquiries monthly
  • 8-15 new jobs completed
  • $1200-3600 additional monthly revenue
  • ROI of 150-300% (your $500 investment generates $1500-3500 in profit)

Most HVAC contractors see results within 30-60 days of consistent marketing effort.
