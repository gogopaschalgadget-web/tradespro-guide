Spring Roofing Checklist: 7 Things Every Homeowner Should Inspect

Published April 07, 2026 | TradesPro Guide

Spring is the best time to inspect your roof before summer storms roll in. Most roofing damage goes unnoticed until it becomes a costly repair. Here are 7 things every homeowner should check.

1. Missing or Damaged Shingles

Walk around your property and look up. Cracked, curled, or missing shingles are the first sign of trouble. Wind damage from winter storms often loosens shingles at edges and ridges.

2. Flashing Around Vents and Chimneys

Metal flashing seals the gaps where your roof meets vertical surfaces. Rust, cracks, or lifted flashing means water is getting underneath. This is the number one cause of interior leaks.

3. Gutter Condition

Clogged or sagging gutters force water back under your roof edge. Clean them out and check that downspouts direct water at least 3 feet away from your foundation.

4. Attic Ventilation

Poor attic ventilation causes moisture buildup that rots decking from the inside. Check for condensation, mold, or a musty smell. Proper ventilation extends roof life by 5 to 10 years.

5. Soffit and Fascia Damage

These boards protect the edges of your roof structure. Peeling paint, rot, or animal damage means moisture is getting in. Repair before it spreads to the decking.

6. Moss and Algae Growth

Green or black streaks on your shingles trap moisture and accelerate deterioration. A zinc strip installed at the ridge line prevents regrowth after cleaning.

7. Interior Water Stains

Check ceilings and walls in the top floor for brown spots or bubbling paint. These indicate active leaks that need immediate attention.

When to Call a Professional

If you find damage in more than one area, get a professional inspection. Most reputable roofers offer free estimates. Get at least 3 quotes and ask for references from your neighborhood.
